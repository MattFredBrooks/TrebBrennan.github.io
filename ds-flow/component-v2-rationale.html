Accessibility

Underline on hover

Buttons have an underline on hover in order to address WCAG2.0 Criterion 1.4.1

1.4.1 Use of Color: Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information, indicating an action, prompting a response, or distinguishing a visual element. (Level A)

Note Focus state is not underlined as it is conveyed using the global focus (orange outline) state.



Round corners

It was discovered during usability observation that the soft buttons and text inputs could sometimes be mistaken for layout or design elements. The border-width was increased from 1px to 3px and the border-radius was added to increase the the affordance.

Note The border-radius is removed on focus. This is because the focus outline does not receive the radius.

Note The border-radius is not supported in IE8.



Disabled button contrast

Disabled Buttons do not need to meet the colour contrast ratio outlined in WCAG2.0 Criterion 1.4.3 as they are not inactive.

1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum): The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1, except for the following: (Level AA)
… Incidental: Text or images of text that are part of an inactive user interface component, that are pure decoration, that are not visible to anyone, or that are part of a picture that contains significant other visual content, have no contrast requirement.
